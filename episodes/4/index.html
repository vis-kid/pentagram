04 | Randy J. Hunt

Randy J. Hunt | Etsy | Branding | Selling Out | Responsibilities Of Designers | Graceful Decline | Advertisement Attention Dynamics | Thinking In Spectrums | Direction Of Better | Community Dynamics | People Centric | Rethinking Work | Creating Circumstances | Design Ecosystem | Rethinking Commerce | Opportunity Costs Of Attention | Not Wanting More | Real Estate Speculation | Data Informed Design Approach

Questions:

  • I got the impression that the DNA of your later works can be traced back a little bit to an early project of yours, “The Amazing Project”. Maybe we could start there…

  • Was there any particular experience, maybe years prior, something that made you do this?

  • How did you not loose that motivation in the professional, in the real world?

  • During your Etsy years, in terms of designer easter eggs, what was your personal attitude or philosophy that you maybe secretly ingrained into Etsy without other people knowing too much? As Design VP you must have had a lot of opportunities to leave some personal DNA embedded in the company…

  • I was always wondering where that endearing quality of Etsy came from. Was that a company DNA influence that came down from founders and the innermost core at Etsy, or was that more of a strategic positioning that was designed from a more rational marketing perspective?

  • What would you say were some key moments in your career that affected your professional trajectory? Do you remember any specific situations that made you rethink your whole approach to work and maybe also life?

  • With Etsy, how would you say did you try to rethink the commerce space and do something new with that?

  • Back then, what was the understanding of what a company like this should be, and in comparison, how different was Etsy at the time?

  • As a designer, when you look around our world, in the small details or in the bigger picture context, what things do you observe that we can and should rethink or redesign right away?

  • For us as humans, society, civilisation in the 21st century, what are we missing that would benefit us all, and that brings us closer together while building a better future?

  • You still live in NY right? When you look around how society works in a place like that, as a designer, what would be the one thing you would recommend should be done differently?

  • How do you keep a fresh perspective on professional matters, and do you use data or some specific methodologies for it?
